Grade 10 Climate

Terms
Weather conditions in a particular area on a particular day
Climate pattern in weather for a region over a long period of time
Global Warming: a rise in the earth’s overall temperature
-CO2 levels and temperature are related
-the temperature rise is causing climates to change around the planet.
Natural Climate change examples -continental drift
-volcanoes
-ocean currents
-the earth’s tilt
-comets and meteorites
Climate change data methods -Tree rings
-coral reef, rock, and glacial layers
Climate change proof -climate change is more aggressive than the past
-rising temperatures
-ice melting
-changes in severe weather, precipitation patterns, and seasons
-ecosystem changes
Heat
Heat a form of energy related to the speed of motion (or vibration) of atoms
Conduction heat transfer by contact
Convection heat transfer through the movement of fluids
Radiation heat transfer through electromagnetic waves
-absorbed by a particle
-transmitted through a particle
-Reflected off a particle
Greenhouse gas
effect		 A phenomenon in which the atmosphere of a planet traps radiation emitted by its sun, caused by gases such as carbon dioxide, water vapor, and methane that allow incoming sunlight to pass through but retain heat radiated back from the planet's surface.
Greenhouse gasses -Burning of fossil fuels
-Cutting or burning trees
-Release of CFC
-Emissions
Heat sinks Hydrosphere and atmosphere
Albedo radiation or light reflected off a surface (the more reflective, the higher the albedo percentage
Earth’s albedo water
Earth’s average albedo 30-40%
heat capacity the amount of heat required to change its temperature by one degree
convection current a current in a fluid that results from convection
carbon sink a forest, ocean
