Periodic table
Period a row of elements in the periodic table.
Group A column of elements in the periodic table with similar properties
Alkali metals (Group 1) soft, highly reactive
Alkaline earth metals (Group 2) light, reactive
Halogens (Group 17) One of the most reactive groups on the periodic table.
Noble gases (Group 18) Very stable, rarely react with any other chemical
Bohr-Rutherford model a model representing the arrangement of electrons in orbits around the nucleus of an atom.

Acids and Bases
Acids
Properties -Sour taste
-Very reactive
-Conduct electricity
-Can act as a catalyst
-Corrosive
-Increased the hydrogen ion (H^1+) concentration of a solution.
-Low pH (less than 7)
Organic acids - found in nature and contain carbon
e.g. vinegar (acetic acid) and ascorbic acids (vitamin C)
Bases
Properties -Bitter taste
-Very reactive
-Conduct electricity
-Caustic
-AKA alkaline
-Increase the hydroxide ion (OH1-) concentration
of a solution
-High pH (greater than 7)
Bases contain... the hydroxide ion (OH1-)
-e.g. sodium hydroxide (NaOH)
bicarbonate looks like an acid but can take a hydrogen ion from water and leave behind. the hydroxide ion
HCO31- + H2O -> H2CO3 + OH1-
Neutralization Reactions acid + base → salt + water
-HCl(acid) + NaOH(base) → NaCl(salt) + H2O
The pH Scale “power of hydrogen”
-Is used to measure the strength of acids and bases
-One unit on the scale represents a 10X change in the strength of the acid or base
