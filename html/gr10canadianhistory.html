Imperialism Should Canada continue to be an important part of Britain’s empire?
Nationalism Should Canada aspire to develop independently of the empire?
Continentalism Should Canada form close relationships with America? Perhaps be annexed by them forming one big nation?
Defining Moments in Foreign Policy
The Naval Issue (1909-1910)Britain need countries’ help to buy newer ships
-Imperialists (English)
-Yes, pay to build new battleships
-Nationalist (French)
-No, it does not affect Canada
Resolution
-None happy
-Laurier established the Royal Canadian Navy, which could be used by the british in case of emergency war
-Imperialists (English):”Tin pot navy”
-Nationalist (French):Too much
The Boer War (1899-1902): British was going to war with Afrikaans in South Africa and requested soldier to help them fight
-English
-Agreed to fight, 23, 000 signatures
-French
-Disagreed to fight
Resolution
-Laurier sent 1000 volunteers
The Alaska Boundary Dispute (1905): 3 judges were sent from each country to discuss where Alaska boundary is.
-United States
-If you do not give we will send army
-British
-We want friendship with US so here you go, too bad Canada
Resolution
-Canadians were furious with the decisions.
-Canada started becoming a more independent country afterwards
Reciprocity (1910-1911): Free trade between Canada and the United States. Get rid of National Policy trade barrier?
-Farmers, Liberals
-yes to free trade
-allows for more mobility
-Conservatives and businesses
-No! National policy keeps our profits high
Resolution
-New Leader of Canada, Robert Borden , was elected
