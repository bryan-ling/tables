The Battles of the First World War
The Second Battle of Ypres Identity
-April 1915
-Belgium
-Defensive battle against a German gas attack
Significance
-First poison gas attack of war
-6, 000 Canadians killed, wounded, captured
-Inspired John McCrae’s “in Flanders Fields”
-Canadians held the line, proving they were capable
Battle of the Somme Identity
-July to November 1916
- Disastrous battle
-Attempt to smash through enemy trenches near the Somme River
Significance
-proved old tactics were outdated (cavalry)
-85% of Royal Newfoundland Regiment destroyed in half an hour
-1 million casualties total
-24, 000 Canadian Casualties
-8km of mud gained
Vimy Ridge Identity
-April 9, 1917
-Canadian attack on a strategic ridge
held by Germans
-Attempted to provide breakthrough
for allies
-new strategies: “rolling barrage” , “Leap frogging”
Significance
-major victory
-distinguished Canada as a nation- “storm troops”
-More ground, prisoners, and weapons taken than so far in the war
-New nationalistic pride for Canada
Passchendaele Identity
-Oct to Nov 1917
-Small ridge in front of muddy fields
-Canadians called on as “storm troops”
-Mud so thick, soldiers and horses drowned in it
Significance
-8, 0000
-small victory with high costs
