Causes of the Great Depressions
Prosperity to Crash -It had seemed the prosperity of the Roaring Twenties would never end
-Business Cycle
-Prosperity, Recession, Depression, Recovery
Black Tuesday -October 29, 1929: the New York stock market crashed
-This followed weeks of instability
-Many people lost their savings overnight
-Many consider the crash to be a cause of the Great Depression, but it was merely the symptom of a sick economy
1 Overproduction and Over-Expansion
-Industries produced too many goods in 1920’s
- Could not sell off inventory
- Production was cut, people lost their jobs
2 Canada’s Dependence on a few Primary Products:
-Canada’s economy depended on staple goods
-In the east, fisheries
-In the west, wheat
-Elsewhere, natural resources
-Global demand fell, so Canada's economy was hit hard
3 -Canada’s economy was closely linked to the U.S.
-When the Depression hit the U.S., Canada suffered as a result
4 Too Much Credit Buying
-Canadians got themselves hopelessly into debt by buying too much on credit
-People spent more than they could afford
5 Buying on Margin
-‘Margin Buying’ meant buying stocks with credit
-Many people thought this was a way to get in on the wealth of the 20’s
-When the stock market crashed, people were left with large debt and worthless stocks
6 High Tariffs Choked Off International Trade
-Higher tariffs were introduced to protect Canadian industries
-Other countries raised tariffs on Canada’s goods as a result
-International trade slowed down as a result
7 Drought
-Little rain for several years meant crop failure in the U.S. and Canadian mid-west
-Many people abandoned their farms because they couldn’t make a living
