Unit 1: Short Stories
Literary devices
Rising action the beginning part of a drama that sets the stage for the climax.
Figurative Language Language that uses figures or speech (such as simile, personification and alliteration) to create imagery.
Imagery is a word or group of words in a literary work which appeal to one or more of the five senses.
Falling Action that part of a drama which follows the climax and leads to the resolution.
Omniscient narrator when the narrator reports on each character’s thoughts and motivations (“all knowing”)
Juxtapose Two or more ideas, places, characters are placed side by side in a narrative or a poem for the purpose of developing comparisons and contrasts
Metaphor takes an identity or concept that we understand clearly and use it to better understand the lesser-known element
Symbolism is using an object or action that means something more than its literal meaning
Hyperbole The device of exaggeration or overstatement. A device often used to create irony, humour or dramatic effect.
Theme is the general idea or insight about the literary piece that a writer wishes to express
Genre is a literary type or form: romance, mystery, sci-fi etc.
Setting is determining Time and Place in fiction
Climax is the turning/decisive point of the story
Euphony is the use of soothing, pleasant sounds
Fiction s the telling of stories which are not entirely based upon facts; imaginary
Cacophony is the use of harsh, unappealing sounding words
Protagonist is the hero or central character of a literary work
Conflict is the struggle found in fiction: external or internal
Pathetic Fallacy A technique of giving a human feeling and/or capacities to nature.
Allusion A direct or indirect reference in one work to another work or to a historical person or event.
Foreshadowing is the use of hints or clues to suggest what will happen later in literature
Oxymoron is putting two contradictory words together (the living dead)
Simile is the indirect comparison of two unlike things using “like” or “as”
Alliteration is the repetition of initial sounds in neighbouring words
Irony An outcome of events contrary to what was, or might have been, expected (Humour or Emphasis)
Onomatopoeia is a word that imitates the sound it represents
Personification is giving human qualities to inanimate objects
Antagonist is a person or force which opposes the protagonist in a literary work
Non-Fiction is an account or representation of a subject which is presented as fact
Flashback is action that interrupts to show an event that happened at an earlier time
How to see a short story
INTRODUCTION a) Setting (when and where)
b) Tone (the flavour of the story)
c) Characters
d) Birth of the plot (what is it all about)
e) Theme (the statement about life, revenge, love, societal expectations)
INITIAL INCIDENT The first conflict or problem that kicks off the rest of the action.
RISING ACTION The rising action introduces complications or obstacles to the protagonist (main character) creating conflict and suspense.
a) conflict develops
b) more characters are introduced
c) sub-plots develop
CLIMAX The most suspenseful moment or the height of the action.
a) the turning point
b) opportunity for the character to resolve the conflict
* the most exciting part of the story
FALLING ACTION The story begins to slowly wind down. Demonstrates the result of the actions or decisions the character has made. This eventually leads to the final part of the novel, the resolution.
RESOLUTION/
DENOUEMENT		 The author ties up loose ends and “wraps it all up.”
TYPES OF CHARACTERS
Protagonist The main / leading character in a novel, story or play
Antagonist The character who struggles or fights against the protagonist.
Secondary Characters People who add to the story without playing a key role.
Confidante someone in whom the central character confides, thus revealing the main character’s personality, thoughts, and intentions.
