Grade 11 Accounting

Accounting: The beginning
Assets something of value owned by a business or a person
Liquidity order order in which assets would be likely converted into cash
Cash(1) -Money related
-Money order: guaranteed payment
Accounts Receivable (2) -purchases
-owed to a business by customers
Office supplies(3) -items used in business office.
Liabilities -debts owed to others by a business or a person
Maturity order payment due date of a liability
Accounts Payable(1) -Total amount owed to creditors
-purchase of goods and services on credit
Bank loan(2) -loans owing to the bank
Mortgage payable(3) Amount borrowed from financial institution to purchase land or buildings
Owner’s Equity A person’s net worth
Accounting equation A = L + OE
Business Entity Principle Requires that each business:
-separate entity
-separate financial data and owner’s personal financial data
Balance sheet -a financial statement which lists the assets, liabilities and owner’s equity at a specific date
Any balance sheet: -who
-what
-when
-accounting equation
Accounting definition -process of keeping track of the daily business activities and summarizing the results in financial reports.
Accounting system -methods used by a business to keep records of its financial activities and to summarize these activities in periodic accounting reports
Purpose of Accounting -to record day to day activities of a business
-to maintain an accurate record of the financial position of a business/individual
-keep detailed record of the changes which occur in the financial position
-To summarize and report information in a useful form for analysis and decision-making
Accounting important to: -owners
-management
-investors
-government
-creditors
ROI Return on Investment
Debtor Individual, Business, or other organization that owes money to a business
Creditor Individual, Business, or other organization to which a business owes a sum of money
Capital Difference between to total assets and the total liabilities; also known as personal equity or net worth.
Personal Net Worth Difference between the cost of the items owned and debts owed
Cost principle Standard practice of recording obtained assets at the actual cost to the business.
