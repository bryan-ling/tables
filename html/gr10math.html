Unit 1: Intro to Quadratic Relations
Degree of a term Sum of the exponents on all variables (in the term)
Degree of polynomial highest degree of all terms in the polynomial
Linear relations modeled by degree 1 polynomials
-1st differences are equal
Quadratic relations modeled by degree 2 polynomials
-1st differences are different and 2nd differences are equal
Parabola a graph of a quadratic relation
Parabola that opens up seconds differences are positive.
Parabola that opens down seconds differences are negative.
Vertex -The point with the lowest y-value if the parabola opens up, (opposite when opens down)
-point where the slope changes sign
Optimal value - the y value of vertex
-maximum
-minimum
Axis of symmetry -vertical line through the vertex
-equation is of the form x = a
Zeros -x intercepts
-can only be 2, 1, or 0 zeros.
Equation of a Parabola in factored form y = a (x - s) (x - t)
If a greater than 0 Parabola opens up
If a less than 0 Parabola opens down
Comparing y=x2 and y=2x
Same - both are curves
- positive slope when x > 0
Different -parabola vs. exponential
-y=x2 has vertex
-parabola is symmetrical
-has negative slope vs. always positive
